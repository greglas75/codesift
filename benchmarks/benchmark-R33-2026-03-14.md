## Key Finding: Single Call = Absolute Minimum Tokens, Unusable Quality

**43,730 tokens** is the absolute minimum achievable with Auggie. But quality at 4.2/10 is unusable.

This establishes the tradeoff curve:

| Calls | Tokens | Quality |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | 43,730 | 4.2/10 |
| 2 | ~51,000 | ~8.0/10 |
| 3 | 57,643 | 8.3/10 |
| 4 | 65,210 | 8.5/10 |

**Optimal point**: 2 calls (R34 = ~51K, 8.0/10). Diminishing returns after 2 calls.

---

## Why Single Call Quality Collapses

Auggie's `codebase-retrieval` is designed for single-concept queries. A 10-task batch query:
1. Returns generically relevant chunks across all topics → diluted results
2. Can't allocate enough "budget" per task → most tasks get 0 relevant chunks
3. The 10-question information_request is too broad for meaningful semantic similarity

**Fix (not feasible):** Would need Auggie to support multi-query batching with per-query allocation, like CodeSift's `queries` array parameter.
