## Key Finding: Ultra-Terse > Form Format for Auggie

| Format | Calls | Tokens | Savings vs R10 |
|--------|-------|--------|----------------|
| Prose (R10) | 4 | 64,148 | baseline |
| Form (R31) | 4 | 65,210 | -1,062 (worse!) |
| **Ultra-terse (R32)** | **3** | **57,643** | **+6,505** |

Ultra-terse format saves 6.5K vs Auggie R10 — but CodeSift R27 is still 6.5K better.

The savings come from:
1. Shorter answer verbosity (ultra-terse more compact than form)
2. Fewer calls (3 vs 4) = less context accumulation (~1-2K)

---

## T9 Fundamental Limitation: Semantic Search Missing ~16 Locations

R32 found 4 production prisma.$transaction locations. R28 (Read/Grep) found 20. Semantic search (both Auggie and CodeSift semantic) cannot reliably find all exact-string matches. Text/BM25 search (CodeSift text type) is required for complete T9 coverage.

---

## Why CodeSift Beats Auggie (Even With Form Answers)

| Factor | CodeSift R27 | Auggie R32 |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Result token control | top_k limits | Uncontrolled — can be verbose |
| T9 coverage | 2-4/10 (test mocks, needs file_pattern fix) | 4/10 (semantic limitation) |
| T7 coverage | 0/10 (recent runs) | 9/10 (semantic finds it!) |
| Answer verbosity | Form saves 18K | Ultra-terse saves 6.5K |
| **Total tokens** | **~51K** | **~58K** |

**Irony:** Auggie is better at T7 (semantic finds error codes well), but CodeSift is better on tokens and most other tasks.
